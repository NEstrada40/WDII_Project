I worked for PLACEHOLDER as Design Lead for Station Salvation II.

Conceptualization and Vision

  • Defined the overarching aesthetic of Station Salvation II, envisioning a digital landscape for players to traverse evolving cyberspace environments.
  • Established the narrative framework, outlining the player's role in combating threats and restoring order to the system.

Gameplay Mechanics

  • Developed a precise aiming system optimized for first-person perspective, allowing players to target enemies and obstacles with ease.
  • Implemented a lock-on targeting mechanism that enabled players to lock onto multiple targets simultaneously, enhancing shooting accuracy.
  • Integrated rhythm-based gameplay mechanics, where player actions such as shooting and movement synchronized with the pulsating soundtrack.

Level Design

  • Designed levels with branching pathways to encourage exploration and replayability, accommodating different player strategies and playstyles.
  • Incorporated environmental hazards and interactive elements strategically to challenge players while maintaining flow and pacing.
  • Utilized dynamic level elements that reacted to player actions, such as environmental changes triggered by defeating enemies or collecting power-ups.

Audiovisual Integration

  • Collaborated with audio designers to implement a dynamic sound system that responded to player interactions and synced seamlessly with the gameplay.
  • Integrated visual effects that complemented the music and gameplay, such as synchronized animations and particle effects triggered by rhythmic cues.
  • Designed immersive visual transitions between levels, leveraging lighting and shader effects to enhance the sense of progression and immersion.
